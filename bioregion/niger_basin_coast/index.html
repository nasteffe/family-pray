Niger Delta and Gulf Coast

Cameroon, Nigeria

Afrotropic

This is the great delta where the mighty Niger river fans out into hundreds of creeks and mangrove swamps before reaching the sea. It is home to Nigeria, Africa's most crowded, busiest, most musical country — and the mangroves are a nursery for more fish than you could count.

How Life Works Here

For a long time, families here lived by fishing the creeks and farming the rich mud. Then oil was found underneath — huge amounts of it — and now it is pumped up and piped away to run cars and factories around the world. Inland, other families grow cocoa for the world's chocolate.

What Is Happening

The oil brought money, but mostly not to the delta families — and worse, leaks and spills have poisoned the creeks, so the fish the people depend on have died. Years ago, a brave man named Ken and others spoke up for their community, the Ogoni, and were killed for it, which shocked the whole world. But the delta did not give up: communities are cleaning the water, replanting the mangroves finger by finger, and young people are still standing up — peacefully — for a clean home.

Something Wonderful

Nigeria makes more films every year than almost any country on Earth — people call it Nollywood. From the same land as the oil and the mangroves comes a river of stories and music that the whole of Africa watches and sings.
