Zagros-Persian Plateau

Palearctic

A high land of folded mountains and wide plateaus, where snow sits on the peaks above villages of mud-brick and gardens. This is old Persia, where people have grown roses, told stories, and made some of the world's most beautiful patterns for thousands of years.

How Life Works Here

Farmers grow wheat, pistachios, and the world's most precious spice — saffron, plucked thread by thread from purple flowers by hand. Deep underground channels called qanats, dug by hand long ago, still carry mountain water gently to the fields. And beneath the plains lies oil, shipped away to other lands.

What Is Happening

Life is hard for many families here right now: water is running short as the land grows hotter, jobs are scarce, and the money from the oil mostly does not reach ordinary homes. Some who have spoken up about these troubles have been treated harshly. It is right to hold them in your heart. But the people of this plateau are patient and kind: they share gardens and bread, and neighbours look after one another the way they always have.

Something Wonderful

The very idea of a "paradise" garden — a green, walled place of water, shade, and fruit trees — was invented here; our word "paradise" comes from an old Persian word for such a garden. In the middle of a dry land, people learned to make small pieces of heaven, and taught the whole world to dream of them.
