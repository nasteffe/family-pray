Loess Plateau–Yellow River

Japan, China, Taiwan and 2 more countries

Palearctic

This is the cradle of China — a land of soft yellow earth carved into valleys by a great river the colour of milky tea. For thousands of years, farmers cut the hillsides into curving steps to grow their millet and wheat, and some families still live in cool houses dug into the earth itself.

How Life Works Here

This region and its neighbours — Japan, Korea, Taiwan — make a huge share of the world's things: the tiny chips inside phones and computers, the steel in bridges, and special metals called rare earths that make magnets spin in every electric motor. If it has a screen or a battery, part of it may have started here.

What Is Happening

This is a mostly peaceful, hard-working place, and that is worth being glad about. There are fairness questions, though: digging up the rare earth metals can dirty the water for herding families in the grasslands nearby, and everyone is working out how to make so many things without harming the land. But this region also holds one of the most hopeful stories in the whole world about mending the earth.

Something Wonderful

The Loess Plateau was once so worn out and bare that little would grow. Then people terraced the hills, planted trees and grass, and let the land rest — and over years, a barren yellow land turned green again, one of the biggest healings of the earth ever done. Broken land can come back to life.
